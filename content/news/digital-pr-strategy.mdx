Digital PR has become one of the highest-leverage things a modern brand can invest in, because the same earned coverage that builds reputation now also drives search authority and AI visibility. Done well, a single strong campaign works across the press, the search results and the answer engines at once.

## What is digital PR?

Digital PR is the practice of earning coverage, mentions and links on online publications, podcasts and platforms to build brand authority and visibility. It blends the storytelling craft of traditional media relations with content, data and search thinking to influence both audiences and the algorithms that shape discovery.

In practice, digital PR turns something newsworthy about your business, a piece of original research, a distinctive point of view, a milestone, a data story, into coverage on sites your audience already reads and trusts. The output is not an advert you paid to place; it is editorial that a journalist chose to publish because it was genuinely interesting to their readers. That distinction is what gives earned coverage its credibility.

What separates digital PR from its analogue predecessor is that the work is built for the web from the start: designed to earn links, to be found in search, and to be quoted by the answer engines that now sit between people and information.

## How is digital PR different from traditional PR?

Traditional PR focuses on reputation and coverage in named outlets; digital PR keeps that goal but adds a broader, measurable scorecard of links, referral traffic, search authority and AI citations. The storytelling craft is shared, the yardstick is wider.

Classic PR has always cared about the quality of coverage and the sentiment around a brand, and those things still matter. Digital PR asks an additional set of questions of every placement: does it carry a link that strengthens our search authority, does it reach the right audience in a measurable way, and does it appear on a source that AI tools trust when they synthesise answers? A mention in a respected outlet is good; a mention that also earns a link and gets cited by an assistant is doing three jobs at once.

The result is a discipline that is more accountable than traditional PR without abandoning its instincts. You still need a real story and a relationship with the journalist. You simply measure the outcome more completely.

## Does digital PR help SEO and AI visibility?

Yes, on both counts. Earned links and brand mentions from credible publications are among the strongest signals of search authority, and that same third-party credibility increasingly determines whether AI answer engines include and trust your brand.

For search, the logic is well established. Links from reputable, relevant sites tell search engines that others vouch for you, and that authority lifts your ability to rank across your whole site, not just the page that was linked. Digital PR is one of the few honest ways to earn those links at scale, because the coverage is genuinely editorial.

For AI visibility, the mechanism is newer but closely related. Generative tools synthesise answers from sources they trust, and consistent mentions across respected publications make your brand look like established fact rather than self-assertion. This is why digital PR and [generative engine optimisation](/news/generative-engine-optimisation-guide) have converged: the coverage that builds search authority is often the same coverage that earns AI citations. The credibility compounds across both channels.

## How do you build a digital PR strategy?

Build a digital PR strategy by defining the audience and outlets that matter, developing genuinely newsworthy stories, and pitching them through real relationships with journalists. Start from what you can credibly say that no one else can, then find the angles that make it a story worth publishing.

The foundation is a clear view of where authority actually helps you. List the publications, podcasts and platforms your buyers trust and that search engines and AI tools respect, and treat coverage on those as the goal rather than sheer volume of mentions. A handful of placements on the right sources will outperform a scattergun of weak ones.

From there, a workable strategy tends to follow this shape:

- Find your angles. Mine proprietary data, strong opinions, customer stories and timely commentary for genuine news hooks.
- Create assets worth covering. Original research and data studies are the most reliable way to earn quality links.
- Build a target list. Identify the specific journalists and outlets aligned to each story.
- Pitch with relevance. Tailor each approach, respect the journalist's beat, and lead with what makes it newsworthy.
- Sustain relationships. Coverage compounds when you become a trusted, quotable source over time.

## How do you measure digital PR?

Measure digital PR against the outcomes it is meant to produce: the quality and relevance of coverage, the links and search authority earned, the referral traffic generated, and increasingly the brand's presence in AI-generated answers. No single metric captures it, so track a small, honest basket.

Vanity counts such as raw mention volume tell you little on their own. What matters is whether the coverage appeared on sources that reach your audience and carry authority, and what that coverage went on to do. A useful reporting picture combines a handful of signals rather than chasing one headline number.

The measures worth watching:

- Quality of placements, judged by relevance and the standing of the outlet.
- Earned links and their contribution to domain and page authority.
- Referral traffic and the behaviour of visitors who arrive through coverage.
- Movement in branded search demand as awareness grows.
- Presence and accuracy of your brand in AI answers on priority topics.

## The takeaway

Digital PR earns the third-party credibility that now powers reputation, search and AI visibility at the same time. Start from a real story, target the sources that genuinely matter, build the journalist relationships that get you covered, and measure the outcomes end to end. It is one of the few investments that pays off across every channel a modern brand cares about.
